Flash cards for fast and effective learning

What are nutrients?


1 marks

The components of food that are necessary for growth and development of our body are called nutrients.

Which are the nutrients essential for our body?


1 marks

Carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, minerals, roughage and water are the nutrients essential for our body.

What is adaptation?


1 marks

The change in specific features or certain habits, which enables a plant or an animal to live in its surroundings is called adaptation.

What is habitat?


1 marks

The surrounding where organisms survive, flourish and reproduce is called a habitat.

Define rest and motion.


1 marks

An object that does not change its positions with time is said to be at rest. An object that changes its positions with time is said to be in motion.

Give any difference between a cell and a battery.


1 marks

A cell has only one plate as a positive and only one plate as a negative electrode, while a battery, which is a combination of cells in a series, can have many plates.

Why does a cell stop producing electricity after some time?


1 marks

An electric cell produces electricity from chemicals stored inside it. When the chemicals inside the cell are used up, the cell stops producing electricity.

What is atmosphere?


1 marks

Our earth is surrounded by a thin layer of air. This layer is called atmosphere. It extends up to many kilometres above the surface of the earth.

What are herbs?


1 marks

The small plants with tender, green, short stem are called herbs. Their stems are not woody and can be bent. A herb may or may not have branches, e.g., tomato, mint.

How many types of dustbins are formed for convenience?


1 marks

There are two types of dustbins: The green colour dustbin is used for biodegradable wastes. The blue colour dustbin is used for non-biodegradable wastes.

What are luminous objects?


1 marks

Luminous objects: Objects that give out or emit light of their own, e.g., sun, torch, light, bulbs, etc.

Why rivers originating from Himalayas do not dry during summer?


1 marks

Rivers originating from the Himalayas do not dry up during summers because they continue to get water from the melting snow on the mountains.

What is fabric?


1 marks

Fabric means a woven material, a textile or other material resembling woven cloth. Fabric is made up of yarns and made by the two main processes known as weaving and knitting.

What is known as golden fibre?


1 marks

Jute is the cheapest natural fibre and is known as the golden fibre.

What are climbers and creepers?


1 marks

In some plants the stem is so weak that it cannot hold it straight. They either stand up with some support or they just spread on the ground and are called climbers and creepers respectively.
