Flash cards for fast and effective learning

What are non-metals?


1 marks

Substances which are soft, non-lustrous, non-sonorous, non-ductile, non-malleable and poor conductor of heat and electricity are called non-metals.

What do you mean amplitude?


1 marks

Vibrations produced by vibrating body in one second is known as amplitude.

What is frequency?


1 marks

The number of oscillations per second is called frequency. Its unit is hertz.

What is habitat?


1 marks

The surrounding where organisms survive, flourish and reproduce is called a habitat.

Give any difference between a cell and a battery.


1 marks

A cell has only one plate as a positive and only one plate as a negative electrode, while a battery, which is a combination of cells in a series, can have many plates.

Why does a cell stop producing electricity after some time?


1 marks

An electric cell produces electricity from chemicals stored inside it. When the chemicals inside the cell are used up, the cell stops producing electricity.

What is atmosphere?


1 marks

Our earth is surrounded by a thin layer of air. This layer is called atmosphere. It extends up to many kilometres above the surface of the earth.

What are herbs?


1 marks

The small plants with tender, green, short stem are called herbs. Their stems are not woody and can be bent. A herb may or may not have branches, e.g., tomato, mint.

How many types of dustbins are formed for convenience?


1 marks

There are two types of dustbins: The green colour dustbin is used for biodegradable wastes. The blue colour dustbin is used for non-biodegradable wastes.

What is meant by atmospheric pressure?


1 marks

The weight of air acting per unit area is known as atmospheric pressure.

What happens when magnesium is burnt in air?


1 marks

Magnesium burns with a white dazzling flame and a white powdery magnesium oxide is formed.

What is a crop?


1 marks

Crop is the term used to describe a plant that is grown in a field on a large scale. For example, cereal crops, wheat crops.

What do you mean by oscillatory motion?


1 marks

The to and fro motion of an object is called vibration. This motion in both the direction from its mean position is called oscillatory motion.
