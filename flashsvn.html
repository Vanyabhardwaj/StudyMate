Flash cards for fast and effective learning

What is villi?


1 marks

The finger like projections in the inner walls of the small intestine is called villi. Function: The villi increase the surface area for absorption of the digested food.

Where is the bile produced? How does it helps?


1 marks

Bile is produced in liver. The bile juice stored in sac called the gall bladder. It helps in the digestion of fats.

Explain how soil is formed.


1 marks

Soil is formed through the process of weathering, i.e. physical breakdown and chemical decomposition of rocks and minerals near or at the surface of the earth.

Name the source from which litmus solution is obtained.


1 marks

Litmus solution is extracted from lichens. It is used to determine whether the given solution is acidic or basic.

How would you verify if the water acidic, basic or neutral?


1 marks

We can verify it by showing that neither blue nor red litmus paper changes its colour when dipped in it.

Name the elements that determine the weather of a place.


1 marks

The temperature, humidity, rainfall, wind-speed, etc. are called the elements that determine the weather of a place.

When are the maximum and minimum temperature likely to occur during the day?


1 marks

The maximum temperature of the day occurs generally in the afternoon and the minimum temperature occurs in the early morning.

Why is transport of materials necessary in a plant or an animal?


1 marks

Because the nutrients and oxygen are made available to all the parts of the body. If the transport of necessary nutrients and oxygen does not take place, the body will not be able to survive.

Name any two effects of electric current.


1 marks

(i) Electric current can give rise to heating and lighting. (ii) Electric current can convert a straight conductor into a temporary magnet.

1 marks

More layers have a lot of space between them which gets filled up with air. Air is a bad conductor, it does not allow the body heat to escape out.

What are stomata?


1 marks

There are small openings on the lower surface of the leaves. These pores are called stomata. These openings are surrounded with guard cells.
